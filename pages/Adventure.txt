      <div id="mw-content-text" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr" lang="en">The <i><b>adventure novel</b></i>
 is a literary genre of novels that has adventure, an exciting 
undertaking involving risk and physical danger, as its main theme. 
Adventure has been a common theme since the earliest days of written 
fiction. <p style="text-align: right; font-size: 84%">—Excerpted from <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Adventure_novel&amp;oldid=115506942">Adventure novel</a> on <i>Wikipedia</i>, the Free Encyclopedia.</p>
<p>The adventure genre overlaps with a number of other genres, notably <a href="https://www.gutenberg.org/wiki/Science_Fiction_(Bookshelf)" title="Science Fiction (Bookshelf)">science fiction</a>, <a href="https://www.gutenberg.org/wiki/Fantasy_(Bookshelf)" title="Fantasy (Bookshelf)">fantasy</a>, <a href="https://www.gutenberg.org/wiki/Pirates,_Buccaneers,_Corsairs,_etc._(Bookshelf)" title="Pirates, Buccaneers, Corsairs, etc. (Bookshelf)">sea novels</a> and <a href="https://www.gutenberg.org/wiki/Western_(Bookshelf)" title="Western (Bookshelf)">westerns</a>. The list presented here is confined to fictional adventure stories that do not fit easily into these other genres.
</p>
